ink-board Enclosure — Model Description
========================================

Purpose
-------
A bottom-less 3D-printed box that houses the ink-board ePaper device.
Printed upside-down (open face on the build plate); no support material required.
The display window sits on a 45°-angled face cut from the top-front-long edge so
the screen faces the viewer at a comfortable diagonal rather than pointing straight up
or straight forward.


Dimensions (all in mm)
-----------------------
  Outer box         L=160  W=80  H=80
  Wall thickness    2.5 mm (all sides and top; bottom is open)
  Chamfer leg       30 mm  →  angled face width = 30 × √2 ≈ 42.4 mm
  Display slot      70 mm (along L) × 30 mm (across angled face)
  Outer edge fillet 1.5 mm radius on all visible outer edges


Geometry concept
----------------
Cross-section in the YZ plane (short-end view):

     ___________
    /            |   ← 45° angled face (chamfer leg = 30 mm each side)
   |             |
   |             |   total height = 80 mm
   |_____________|   ← open bottom (Z = 0)

  Y: front of box = Y=0; rear = Y=80
  Z: bottom (open) = Z=0; top = Z=80

The angled face runs the full 160 mm length.
Its surface normal points in the [0, −1, +1] / √2 direction (forward-upward).
The face center in world coordinates: X=80, Y=15, Z=65.

A 70 × 30 mm rectangular window is cut perpendicularly through this face,
centered both along the 160 mm length and across the 42.4 mm face width.


Parameter table
---------------
  L          160    outer length (mm)
  W           80    outer width  (mm)
  H           80    outer height (mm)
  wall_t       2.5  wall thickness (mm)
  chamfer     30    45° chamfer leg length (mm); face width = chamfer × √2
  slot_l      70    display slot length along L (mm)
  slot_w      30    display slot width across face (mm)
  slot_cut    15    slot cutter depth (mm); must exceed face wall thickness (~3.5 mm)
  fillet_r     1.5  outer edge fillet radius (mm)
  $fn         64    circle/sphere resolution


OpenSCAD module structure
-------------------------
  filleted_box(l, w, h, r)   Solid box with filleted vertical edges and rounded top
                              corners; bottom rim stays sharp. Built with hull() of
                              cylinders (vertical edges) and spheres (top corners).

  outer_shell()               Hollow shell: filleted_box minus inner cavity.
                              Inner cavity starts at Z=0 (open bottom), leaves
                              wall_t on all five closed sides.

  chamfer_wedge()             Triangular prism removed from top-front-long edge.
                              Built via rotate([0,90,0]) + linear_extrude of a
                              right-triangle polygon — NOT polyhedron (see pitfalls).
                              Coordinate mapping: local_x = −world_z, local_y = world_y;
                              extrusion axis → world X.

  display_slot()              Rectangular cutter (slot_l × slot_w × slot_cut) centered
                              on the angled face. Placed at the face center
                              (L/2, chamfer/2, H−chamfer/2), then rotate([45,0,0])
                              aligns the cut perpendicular to the face.

  Main:  difference() { outer_shell(); chamfer_wedge(); display_slot(); }


Known design issue (slot spans full face)
-----------------------------------------
The outer_shell() inner cavity extends all the way to the chamfer region. After
chamfer_wedge() removes the top-front-long corner, the only material on the angled
face is the 2.5 mm shell wall — ~3.54 mm thick diagonally. From the front the entire
chamfer area appears as a large opening; the 70×30 mm slot is indistinguishable.

Fix approach: the angled face needs to be a solid panel with only the slot as the
opening. Either:
  (a) Clip the inner cavity so it does not extend past Z = H−chamfer (z=50), giving
      a solid block behind the face, or
  (b) Add a chamfer_fill() module that re-adds solid material at the face location
      after the main difference, then subtract only the display slot through it.


Print orientation
-----------------
Model origin: open bottom at Z=0.
Place on build plate with Z=0 face down (box printed "upside-down").
The 45° angled face overhangs at 45° — within the typical no-support threshold.
All other faces are vertical or horizontal.
